When I was 14 I played in <em>The Sorzls</em> and our school band. Before I started playing the bass I honestly didn't know what it was. My best friend Ole recently bought a guitar and our plans to start a band demanded a bass player. I remember how he showed me pieces of System Of A Down in which the bass had prominent parts. When he indicated the beginning of the bass riff with his raised eye brows and his finger pointing to the speakers I nodded to imply that I heard what he meant. I didn't. Even when we went to the local music store in Frankfurt I still couldn't tell the difference. However, I realized the thickness of the strings and understood how a bass can be played with the fingers.

When I bought a beginner's bass with my own saved money and came through our flat's door my parents were shocked. They weren't happy about my decision to make music. They were/are very conservative and associated music with being a good-for-nothing. It was even worse that I then liked metal music.

But, I just continued and endured my parents disapproval. After <em>The Sorzls</em> we found <em>notenoughspace</em> to be our own project without any school backing us up. The name of our band resulted from the size of our rehearsal room.
